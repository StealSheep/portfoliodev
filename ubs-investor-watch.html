Website design

Improving engagement

UBS' flagship publication, Investor Watch, was intended as a client-facing publication that communicated market trends and investor sentiment in an easy-to-digest format.

When I came on board at WMA, researching the numbers, it was not meeting its intended goals. Engagement was low and production time was high. It failed to follow conventional design patterns that users had come to expect in favor of unneccsarily complex, hard-to-follow layouts and animations and movement that didn't enhance the experience, only distract from it. Accessibility was also an major issue as the team was using mostly graphics to convey textual information with no thought to alternate browsing and input methods.

Working with the research teams and the writing staff, we brought Investor Watch more closely into brand, created a user journey from the intial touch points (email, social media, etc.) through the final conversion that was as friction-free as possible.

Graphically we developed a visual style that broadened the brand while making the content snackable and easy to understand at a glance.

The process for creating the publication was also dramatically streamlined resulting in an much higher return on investment.

When relaunched, the new Investor Watch saw not only a significant uptick in overall visits but engagement went through the roof. What used to be only a few hundred visitors who bounced almost immediately became ultimately tens of thousands per release, the vast majority of whom engaged with the content in full leading to an uptick in conversions (i.e., reach-outs to financial advisors).
